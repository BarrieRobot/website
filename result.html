The demo machine.

We, as a team, worked hard to present the advertised next generation bartender. The finalised robot was demoed in the hall of the Science Centre on the 18th of January 2018. This pages features some images, remarks, reviews and results of that day.

Several things broke down on the demo day, but the show had to go on. Barrie was eventually cured of his illnesses an hour before launch, and could welcome the visitors. Much has been learned from the feedback from the people that used Barrie during the day. These points will all be taken in consideration if the robot will eventually undergo further development. Despite the last minute problems and thanks to many late hours, the robotic bartender has become a success. Overall, we are proud of what we have accomplished.

Press coverage

Multiple different press channels were present at the demo day to take a look at the TU Delft's robotic creations.

The Science Centre Delft is our employer. The Science Centre has the ambition to supply visitors an experience and education concerning new technologies developed at the TU Delft. Their vision is to have multiple robots in the building that assist and entertain the visitor. These robots should communicate with each other to give each user their own, personal experience.

Apart from our robot, another group is concurrently building a host robot. Its purpose is to provide information to visitors entering or leaving the building. See their project page, IONA. Other robots are meant to be added to the system later.
